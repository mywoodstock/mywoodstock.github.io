select research projects

Parallel algorithms for sequence alignments on the Cell processor Two prominent parallel algorithms for sequence alignments exist. One uses the wavefront pattern, and the other is based on parallel-prefix. A comparison of these algorithms for performance on a cheap high-performance processor, the Cell BE, leads to a hybrid algorithm based on these two approaches. This scheme gives speedups of four on a Playstation 3 (six SPEs) compared to a Pentium 4 processor. Furthermore, it gives speedups of seven on a QS20 Cell blade (sixteen SPEs). These hold for the three varieties of alignments – global, spliced and syntenic.
